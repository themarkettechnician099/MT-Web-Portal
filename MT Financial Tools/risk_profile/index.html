The Guidebook

Master your portfolio, understand the mechanics, and grow your wealth.

Part 1: The Math of Your Life

Investing is not about predicting the future, and it is certainly not about how brave you feel. It is about understanding the mathematical reality of your current life.

The stock market is inherently volatile. At some point, your portfolio will experience a severe drop. Before you invest a single peso, we have to answer one vital question: If the market crashes tomorrow, will your real-life financial obligations force you to sell your investments at a loss?

To answer this, we calculate your Structural Capacity. By looking at your age, your emergency cash, your income stability, and your time horizon, we determine exactly how much market volatility your life can safely absorb.

The Core Compounding Principle

Once your capacity is calculated, control shifts to your discipline. Wealth is built through a universal formula:

$$Equity_n = Equity_0 \times (1 + r)^n$$
  • $Equity_0$ = Your starting capital (plus your consistent monthly additions).
  • $r$ = Your average annual rate of return.
  • $n$ = Time (number of years your money remains invested).

Notice that time ($n$) is the exponent—it is the most powerful variable in the equation. To let time do its work, you must be in a portfolio that you can comfortably hold for decades without losing sleep. That is why we provide a Risk Thermostat on your dashboard. While the system calculates your maximum mathematical capacity, you can always use the thermostat to safely dial down your risk to match your emotional comfort level.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why did the system assign me a "Conservative" profile when I want to invest aggressively?

Because there is a massive difference between Risk Tolerance (how brave you feel) and Risk Capacity (how much money you can afford to lose). If you are 60 years old and retiring soon, or need the money in 2 years, your capacity to recover from a crash is near zero. The system restricts you to protect your principal.

Q: Why can I lower my risk on the Thermostat, but I can't raise it?

Think of the assignment as a structural speed limit. You can safely drive up to it. However, if you prefer less stress, you are allowed to drive slower. Emotion should be allowed to downgrade your risk for peace of mind, but it should never allow you to take more risk than your finances support.

Q: I have 5 years of experience, but it locked me out of "Third Liners". Why?

Experience only unlocks speculative assets if your foundation allows for it. If you have 5 years experience but a 3-year time horizon, experience cannot save you from a sudden crash. Speculation requires both skill (Mastery) and the financial safety net to absorb total loss (Capacity).

Q: The chart assumes my top-up is static for 40 years. Won't income go up?

Yes, ideally. But human behavior shows "lifestyle creep" often consumes raises. We keep it static to brutally demonstrate that if you do not actively increase your savings rate over time, 4% inflation will eventually eat your contributions alive.
