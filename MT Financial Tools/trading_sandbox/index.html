The Guidebook

Master your portfolio, understand the mechanics, and grow your wealth.

Part 1: The Purpose

Trading is not magic; it is raw, geometric math. This simulator is not a crystal ball, but a mathematical mirror designed to strip away emotion. It reveals the inescapable reality of your current trading habits over a large sample size. It exists to answer one vital question: If you keep trading exactly like this, will you go bankrupt or compound wealth?

Part 2: The Pillars & Operations

1. Dashboard (The Command Center)

Purpose: A visual health check of your entire system.
Operation: Monitors live capital deployment, exposes your structural asymmetry via distribution charts, and tracks active holdings.

2. Trading Plan (The Allocator)

Purpose: Forces risk definition and position sizing before entry.
Operation: The Dual-Cap Sizing Engine protects you by enforcing the lesser of your VaR (Risk %) or your absolute Position Limit.

3. Ledgers (The Bank & History)

Purpose: The immutable truth of cash flow and operations.
Operation: Logs all deposits/withdrawals and records your closed trades for historical performance calculations.

4. Projections (Expectancy Forecaster)

Purpose: Projects your historical edge into the future.
Operation: Forecasts geometric compounding, calculates Exact Time to Goal, and identifies mathematical risk of ruin.

Part 3: Defining Your Edge

An edge is not a prediction. It is a statistical advantage expressed over a series of trades, not a single outcome. Any individual trade is random; only the distribution of many trades reveals whether a system is profitable. The purpose of this section is not to simplify edge into a slogan, but to define the mechanics that sustain it.

At its core, edge is governed by the relationship between outcomes—how often you win, how much you gain, how much you lose, and how much capital you expose per attempt. These variables interact continuously, and small distortions in one can invalidate the entire system.

Core Expectancy Framework (Universal Model)

$$ E = (P_w \times G) - (P_l \times L) $$

Where:
\( P_w \) = Probability of winning
\( P_l \) = Probability of losing
\( G \) = Average gain
\( L \) = Average loss

1. Edge is Statistical, Not Situational

Edge does not exist at the level of a single trade. It only becomes visible over a large sample.

2. Asymmetry Defines Profitability

Profitability emerges from imbalanced outcomes, not accuracy. Losing 1 unit while gaining 2–3 units creates structural advantage. This allows profitability even with a sub-50% win rate. However, asymmetry is only effective if losses remain controlled.

Part 4: The Wealth Playbook

Once a trade is placed, control shifts. The market determines outcome; the trader only defines risk and response. Wealth, therefore, is not built by prediction, but by maintaining a system that can endure repeated uncertainty without failure.

Core Compounding Principle

$$ Equity_{n} = Equity_{0} \times (1 + r)^n $$

1. Control What Is Actually Controllable

After entry, only two variables remain: Position size and Exit discipline.

2. Understand Drawdown as a Structural Cost

Drawdowns are not failures; they are embedded in the system. Losing streaks will occur. The system must be designed to absorb these periods without requiring adjustment.
